The Problem

Indian commercial parking runs on chaos — and slot-level systems make it worse.

01

Morning loop

Everyone arrives 8–9:30 AM. Gates back up, cars circle blind, lanes double-park.

02

Slots don't work

You cannot make an Indian driver park in "Slot 143." Enforcement fails, so the data goes stale instantly.

03

Blind managers

No live view of utilisation, peaks, or which user class is saturating which level.

04

No signal underground

Lower basements have no cellular. Cloud- or phone-dependent systems fail exactly where it matters.

02 / 13
The Solution

One good decision at the entrance — and we stick with it.

  • The entrance camera reads the number plate.
  • The on-site engine picks the best floor + zone from live occupancy.
  • A display + printed ticket show it. A QR opens an optional map.
  • The driver parks in any free slot inside that zone.
  • No re-routing. No app. No enforcement.
Zones, not slots. Memorable, human, and impossible to break.

Computer vision counts cars per zone so the next decision stays accurate. The cloud is for analytics only — it can fail and the lot keeps running.

04 / 13
Product Details

Three layers. Only one of them needs the internet.

GATE LAYER

Ground level

LPR camera, entrance display, ticket printer, QR generator. Sits where signal is good and renders the decision.

EDGE LAYER

On-premise · offline

CV occupancy, mmWave radar ingestion, the allocation algorithm, hold management, signage. Internet drops, parking continues.

CLOUD LAYER

Analytics only

Dashboards, reports, history, multi-site analytics. Never on the live loop — its failure cannot stop parking.

CV answers exactly one question: "How many cars are in each zone?" — no tracking, no trajectories, no lane-speed, no re-routing.
